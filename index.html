Pokémon FireRed — Complete Playthrough Guide

Welcome, Trainer! This guide walks you from the first steps in Pallet Town to becoming Champion of Kanto — covering every Gym, route, and secret area. Just scroll down and follow along.

Starting Out — Pallet Town to Viridian City

After choosing your starter (Bulbasaur, Charmander, or Squirtle), collect Oak’s Parcel from Viridian City Poké Mart and return it to receive the Pokédex. Stock up on Poké Balls and catch early Pokémon on Route 1 and 2. Your rival will challenge you on Route 22 — make sure to level up first.

Pewter City — Brock’s Gym

Viridian Forest is your first major area — catch a Pikachu or Bug-type here. In Pewter City, train to level 12–14 before facing Brock. Water and Grass Pokémon dominate this battle. You’ll earn the Boulder Badge and TM39 (Rock Tomb).

Cerulean City — Misty’s Gym

Pass through Mt. Moon, where you can obtain fossils and catch Clefairy or Geodude. Misty specializes in Water Pokémon, so bring Electric or Grass-types. Winning nets you the Cascade Badge and TM03 (Water Pulse).

Vermilion City — Lt. Surge’s Gym

Board the S.S. Anne to obtain HM01 (Cut). Lt. Surge uses Electric Pokémon — counter them with Diglett or other Ground-types. Victory rewards you with the Thunder Badge and TM24 (Thunderbolt).

Celadon City — Erika’s Gym

Defeat Team Rocket in the Game Corner’s hidden base. Erika’s Grass-types fall easily to Fire, Flying, or Ice moves. You’ll receive the Rainbow Badge and TM19 (Giga Drain).

Fuchsia City — Koga’s Gym

The Safari Zone grants HM03 (Surf) and HM04 (Strength). Koga’s Poison Pokémon are weak to Psychic or Ground moves. Earn the Soul Badge and TM06 (Toxic).

Saffron City — Sabrina’s Gym

Defeat Team Rocket in Silph Co. to unlock this Gym. Sabrina’s Psychic Pokémon are powerful — Dark, Ghost, or strong Physical Pokémon help here. Reward: Marsh Badge and TM04 (Calm Mind).

Cinnabar Island — Blaine’s Gym

Revive fossils at the Pokémon Lab. Blaine’s Fire-types are best handled by Water or Rock Pokémon. You’ll earn the Volcano Badge and TM38 (Fire Blast).

Viridian City — Giovanni’s Gym

Giovanni’s Ground Pokémon return for one final match. Grass or Water Pokémon secure victory for the Earth Badge and TM26 (Earthquake). The path to the Elite Four opens!

Pokémon League — The Elite Four

The toughest challenge awaits in Indigo Plateau:

  • Lorelei — Ice types; use Electric or Fighting moves.
  • Bruno — Rock/Fighting types; Psychic or Flying moves help.
  • Agatha — Ghost/Poison; Psychic or Ground moves are key.
  • Lance — Dragons; use Ice and Rock moves.
  • Champion Rival — A mixed team; adapt and strike wisely.

Side Quests & Legendary Pokémon

  • Power Plant — Catch Zapdos, the Electric Bird.
  • Seafoam Islands — Solve puzzles to reach Articuno.
  • Victory Road — A final test filled with items and strong trainers.
  • Mt. Ember — Post-game location with Moltres.
  • Lost Cave — Find Selphy and earn rare items.

Tips for Success

  • Always keep diverse types to counter every Gym.
  • Use Repels in caves to save time.
  • Save before important battles or legendaries.
  • Use status effects (Sleep, Paralysis) when catching Pokémon.
  • Balance offense and defense — don’t overtrain one Pokémon.
